Introduction


All things are numbers.

Mathematics has been one of the foundational pillars of human knowledge and scientific inquiry since ancient times. Its applications range from the study of the natural world to the workings of the human mind, and its impact can be felt across nearly every academic discipline. However, the true value of mathematics lies not only in its inherent elegance and complexity but also in its ability to facilitate interdisciplinary collaboration and knowledge exchange.

In today's increasingly interconnected world, interdisciplinary research and collaboration have become essential for addressing complex problems and driving innovation across a variety of fields. Mathematics, with its unique ability to provide a common language and framework for understanding complex phenomena, has emerged as a crucial tool in interdisciplinary research. By providing a rigorous and systematic approach to problem-solving, mathematics can help researchers bridge the gap between different disciplines, enabling them to communicate more effectively and work towards common goals.

This seminar centers on the intersection of mathematics with the domains of materials and mechanical engineering.
